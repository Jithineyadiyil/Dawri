1. Overview

Dawri Platform ("Dawri", "we", "us") provides esports tournament hosting, a digital goods marketplace, and workplace engagement tools. This policy explains what personal information we collect when you use our website or mobile apps, why we collect it, and the rights you have over that information under the Saudi Personal Data Protection Law ("PDPL") and applicable regional laws.

If you use Dawri through an employer, university, or partner organization ("Enterprise Customer"), that organization may also have its own privacy notice. Where the Enterprise Customer is the controller of your data, their policy takes precedence over ours for data shared with them.

2. Information we collect

We collect information in three ways: you provide it directly, we observe it automatically, and we receive it from partners.

You provide:

  • Account information — name, email, mobile number, password, country.
  • Gaming profile — in-game nicknames, PSN ID, PUBG ID, COD ID, preferred games.
  • Payment information — card details processed by our payment gateway (we never store full card numbers), wallet top-ups, billing address.
  • Tournament submissions — match screenshots, result disputes, messages to moderators.
  • Communications — emails, support tickets, survey responses.

We collect automatically:

  • Device data — IP address, browser type, operating system, device identifiers.
  • Usage data — pages visited, tournaments joined, marketplace actions, session duration.
  • Cookies & similar tech — see Section 9.

From partners:

  • Enterprise HR systems — when your employer integrates SAP SuccessFactors, Oracle HCM, or Workday, we receive your employee roster entry (name, email, department).
  • Payment providers — transaction confirmations, chargeback notices.
  • SMS verification — phone carrier verification status via Unifonic / Taqnyat.

3. How we use information

We use your information only for the purposes described below:

  • Running the service — creating your account, letting you register for and compete in tournaments, generating brackets, delivering digital card codes.
  • Payments & billing — processing purchases, wallet top-ups, refunds, and sending invoices.
  • Security & fraud prevention — rate-limiting, SMS OTP verification, detecting cheating in tournaments, investigating abuse of the marketplace.
  • Customer support — responding to your messages and resolving complaints.
  • Analytics & improvement — understanding which features are used, optimizing match scheduling, measuring performance.
  • Legal obligations — responding to lawful requests from Saudi authorities, complying with tax and anti-money-laundering rules.
  • Marketing — only with your consent, and only for Dawri's own services; you can opt out at any time from your account settings.

4. How we share information

We do not sell your personal information. We share it only with the parties below and only to the extent necessary:

  • Gift card distributors — LikeCard, WUPEX, Reloadly, and similar partners receive the minimum data needed to fulfill a marketplace order (order reference, product ID, quantity). They do not receive your email, phone, or payment details.
  • Payment gateways — Moyasar, Tap Payments, or HyperPay receive the card details you enter to process a charge. These are PCI-DSS compliant providers; Dawri does not see or store raw card numbers.
  • SMS & email providers — Unifonic, Taqnyat, and our email platform receive your phone or email solely to deliver a specific message (OTP, order confirmation, match reminder).
  • Enterprise Customers — if you joined via an employer, we share tournament participation and engagement statistics with the HR administrator who organized the event. Individual match screenshots are never shared with HR.
  • Service providers — cloud hosting, error monitoring, and analytics vendors who process data on our behalf under contracts that restrict their use.
  • Legal authorities — where we receive a lawful request or are required by PDPL, court order, or regulator in a jurisdiction where we operate.
  • Business transfers — if Dawri is acquired or merged, personal data may transfer to the successor entity; you will be notified and given the rights described in Section 7.

5. Data retention

We keep your information only as long as we need it:

  • Active accounts — for as long as you maintain an account with us.
  • Closed accounts — 30 days after deletion request, then permanently erased or anonymized (except where legal obligations require longer retention, e.g. tax records kept for 10 years per Saudi ZATCA rules).
  • Marketplace codes — unrevealed codes auto-expire after 30 days; revealed codes are stored encrypted for 12 months for dispute resolution.
  • Match screenshots — retained for the duration of the tournament plus 90 days for dispute windows.
  • Support tickets — retained for 24 months.
  • Server logs — retained for 90 days unless flagged for security investigation.

6. Security

We implement industry-standard controls to protect your information:

  • All traffic to Dawri is encrypted in transit with TLS 1.3.
  • Marketplace card codes are encrypted at rest with AES-256-GCM.
  • Passwords are hashed with bcrypt.
  • SMS OTP is required for account registration, login from new devices, and high-value marketplace purchases.
  • Multi-factor authentication (MFA) is mandatory for administrator accounts and available to all users.
  • Access to production systems is limited to a small, audited engineering team.

No system is perfectly secure. If we learn of a breach that affects your information, we will notify you and the relevant regulator within 72 hours, as required by PDPL.

7. Your PDPL rights

Saudi PDPL grants you the following rights over your personal data:

  • Right to know — request a summary of what data we hold about you and how it is used.
  • Right of access — receive a copy of your data in a portable format.
  • Right to correction — update inaccurate or incomplete information.
  • Right to erasure — request deletion of data that is no longer necessary (subject to legal retention rules in Section 5).
  • Right to object — refuse certain types of processing, including marketing.
  • Right to portability — receive your data in a machine-readable format to transfer elsewhere.
  • Right to withdraw consent — where processing is based on your consent, you may revoke it at any time without affecting prior lawful processing.
  • Right to complain — lodge a complaint with the Saudi Data & Artificial Intelligence Authority (SDAIA) if you believe your rights have been violated.

To exercise any of these rights, email our Data Protection Officer at dpo@dawri.gg. We respond within 30 days.

8. Minors

Dawri is designed for users aged 13 and over. Users under 18 require the consent of a parent or legal guardian to create an account. We do not knowingly collect personal information from children under 13. If you believe a child under 13 has provided us with personal data, email dpo@dawri.gg and we will delete the account and data promptly.

9. Cookies & analytics

We use cookies and similar technologies for three purposes:

  • Strictly necessary — keeping you signed in, remembering your language and region preferences. These cannot be disabled.
  • Analytics — understanding how users navigate the platform. You can opt out in your account settings.
  • Marketing — measuring ad effectiveness when we run promotions. Off by default; enabled only with your explicit consent.

You can also control cookies directly in your browser settings, though some features of Dawri will not work without the strictly necessary cookies.

10. International transfers

Your data is primarily stored on servers located in Saudi Arabia. Some of our service providers (such as cloud hosting and email delivery) process data in other countries. When we transfer data outside Saudi Arabia, we ensure an equivalent level of protection either through PDPL-approved standard contractual clauses or by transferring only to jurisdictions recognized as providing adequate protection.

11. Changes to this policy

We may update this policy as our services evolve. If a change materially affects your rights or how your data is used, we will notify you by email and in-app notification at least 14 days before the change takes effect. The "Last updated" date at the top of this page always reflects the most recent revision.
